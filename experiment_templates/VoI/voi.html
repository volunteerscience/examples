Game Instructions:

During the game, you will use your judgment to rank a series of cards, each card represents a single piece of information available to you while performing your job. The cards are divided into 3 decks: training, emergency, and planning. Each deck, other than training, consists of 40 hands of 5 or 7 cards. The training deck has 3 hands as its only use is to familiarize you with the game. You will rank each card based upon the deck tempo (where) and 3 card attributes: source reliablity (who), information content (what), and latency (when). These values are further explained below.

Deck Tempo:

  • Training: For training purposes only. No extra consideration beyond the card attributes is required.
  • Emergency: The tempo of the emergency deck is an emergency situation, for example a firefighter battling a fire. The situation is volatile, you need up to the minute information and decisions must be made quickly. You must consider these conditions while ranking the cards.
  • Planning: The tempo of the planning deck is long term planning, for example a fire marshal planning a fire prevention campaign. You have the time and resources to consider trends and evaluate information carefully before committing to a plan. Again, be sure to consider these conditions as you rank the cards.

During the game, the 3 decks are accessed in the order training, emergency, and planning. When you are ready to view the first hand in a deck, press the Begin button on the game screen. Once a hand is dealt, this button is grayed out until all the hands in the deck are finished. The red text at the top of the page always reminds you which deck you are currently playing.

To play the hand, consider the attributes on each card and then drag and drop the cards from the Given Data Set column to the Ranked Data Set column to arrange the cards from highest to lowest VoI (see picture below). During this ranking process, you may return all the cards to their original state in the Given Data Set column by pressing the Reset button. This allows you to restart the ranking process. When you are satisfied with the ranking of the hand, you can press the Enter Scores button. This action finishes the data collection on the current hand and deals a new hand (unless the deck is empty). Consequently, the Count box will increment by one to indicate the current hand in play. If the deck is empty, the web application sets up the next deck (unless all decks are finished).
